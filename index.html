Artificial Intelligence at the Edge: Smart Visitor Management System with Facial Recognition (University Final Year Project)

Oct 2019 – Apr 2020
Traditionally, in order to perform artificial intelligence functions on edge devices such as IoT sensors and mobile phones, the application or system had to be hosted on a cloud platform. With the exponential increase of edge devices and data generated, capabilities of cloud computing would eventually be limited by the bandwidth and latency of the network. To mitigate the potential risks posed by cloud computing, this report discusses the possibility to move processing of data to where it is being generated by having Artificial Intelligence at the Edge. To evaluate this new computing paradigm, a visitor management system with facial recognition was implemented. The facial recognition model created was able to achieve an accuracy of 94% on static datasets with an inference speed of less than one second per photo while running on a RaspberryPi. The visitor management system was deployed and tested on both edge and cloud architectures. Results shown that performance of the system is actually better when AI inference is done on the edge.

Cancer is one of the leading causes of mortality in Singapore. Decentralised Smart Home System

Nov 2019 – Dec 2019
With the rapid advancements in smart Internet of Thing devices, popularity in smart home systems has also substantially increased. However, it was found that majority of mainstream smart home systems deploys an architecture that heavily rely on a centralised component for control and communication between sensors and devices. A distributed and decentralised smart home architecture was designed which effectively removes the need of a central hub, eliminating the single point of failure. Java RMI was used to facilitate the implementation of the system, with each component acting as a remote object. The implemented system simulates a smart living room environment, with devices making changes to its settings and states with the real time data collected from sensors. Evaluation was done to test both the system’s functionality and performance, results show that it was able to successfully exhibit expected behaviour based on the defined semantics of the devices as well as handling large amount of invocations without exception.

Twitter Crawler with Name Entity Recognition

Oct 2019 – Nov 2019
With more than 500 million Tweets being sent daily, there is an average of approximately 21 million Tweets, which contain valuable data, being sent by the hour. By analysing these Tweets, it is possible to find out what topics and issues are trending on Twitter at any points in time.
A Twitter Crawler was developed using the Streaming and REST APIs through the implementation of a hybrid architecture to facilitate data collection of Tweets from London. Thereafter, basic data analytics was conducted on a 1-hour time frame of the data retrieved from the crawler. Named Entity Recognition was also performed for advanced social media analytics on the entities extracted.

Customer Loyalty Management System for Hospitality Industry

Sep 2018 – Mar 2019
Developed an online platform that enables Hotels, Hotel Chains and Corporate Groups to attract potential guest and improve retention rates. The platform was split into two separate systems, a member access portal, and an administrator access portal.
The member access portal is the system catered for hotel guests. It improves a guest’s overall experience with the hotel by allowing them to view their stay history, make reservations, earn loyalty points and receive special promotions.
The administrator access portal allows the hotel’s sales and marketing team to better maintain guest relation by providing them with a platform to review past and current guest information.
